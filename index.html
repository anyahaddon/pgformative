Slow vs Fast Fashion

The term fast fashion is thrown around a lot online these days - but what does it actually mean? Fast fashion usually describes the conventional business model of mass producing high fashion trends at low cost, at the detriment of workers and the planet.

Slow fashion refers to a fashion awareness and approach that takes into account the procedures and resources needed to create apparel. It promotes the purchase of higher-quality clothes that will last longer, as well as equitable treatment of people, animals, and the environment. (Wikipedia, 2021, Slow Fashion, viewed 2 November 2021, )

It is an alternative to fast fashion in the sense that it promotes a more ethical and sustainable way of living and consuming. (Hill, M, 2021, "What Is Slow Fashion?", Good On You, viewed 2 November 2021, )

Unlike fast fashion, slow fashion production ensures quality manufacturing to lengthen the life of the garment or material. In accordance with the slow movement there is a trend towards more conscious buying as well as companies attracting new consumers with their eco-friendly processes. (Wikipedia, 2021, Slow Fashion, viewed 2 November 2021, )

Top tips to make your wardrobe more sustainable

Buy less

Even the greenest garment uses resources for production and transport to your home, creating some environmental impact.

Buy better quality

If we stop buying poor quality, it will push brands to improve the quality of their garments. It will also allow us to keep our clothes longer, which is good for our wallets and for the environment.

Sustainable washing

The average household does almost 400 loads of laundry every year, consuming about 60,000 liters of water.

(Sustain your Style, 2020, How can we reduce our Fashion Environmental Impact?, viewed 3 November 2021)
